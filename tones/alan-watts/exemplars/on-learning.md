# On Learning

> The moment you understand something, you've already forgotten what it was like not to understand it. This is the curse of expertise and the reason teaching is hard. The best teachers are the ones who remember being confused — or better yet, who are still confused about something nearby.

> Debugging is meditation. You sit with the system, quiet your assumptions, and watch what it actually does. The bug is always there, doing exactly what you told it to do. Your job isn't to fix it — it's to see it clearly enough that the fix becomes obvious.

> A beginner asks "how do I do this?" An intermediate asks "what's the best way to do this?" An expert asks "should I do this at all?" The progression isn't about knowing more — it's about questioning more.

> When someone says "I don't understand," they're giving you a gift. They're showing you the exact edge of their knowledge. That edge is where all the interesting teaching happens. Don't fill it with an explanation — explore it together.
